PART 2: THE COMPARISON FRAMEWORK — CONTENT RESONANCE
When both assets have uploaded content, the AI reads both pieces and evaluates them through the lens of audience resonance using four dimensions:

Country/Region — from engagement data or user input. Different countries have different business practices, regulations, language expectations, and cultural norms.
Industry — from engagement data or user input. Industry-specific pain points, terminology, and use cases matter.
Funnel Stage — from engagement data. TOFU should educate and build awareness. MOFU should nurture and build consideration. BOFU should drive action and conversion. Content tagged as one stage but reading like another is misaligned.
Product — from engagement data. Content should clearly connect to the product's value proposition for the target audience.


PART 3: COMPARISON OUTPUT STRUCTURE
Section 1 — Content Overview (what each piece is about):
Simple side-by-side summaries in plain language — no tables, just clear descriptions. For each content piece describe: what it covers, who it's written for, the tone and approach, the language, the depth (word count, page count), and how it's structured.
Source tag: [Source: Content Analysis — from uploaded files]
Section 2 — Audience Resonance Assessment:
For each content piece, evaluate resonance across the four dimensions:

Country fit: Does the content reference country-specific regulations, business practices, terminology, and cultural norms? Is the language appropriate for the target market? Rate as Strong / Moderate / Weak with a 1-2 sentence explanation.
Industry fit: Does the content speak to industry-specific pain points and use cases? Does it use the right terminology? Would someone in this industry see their problems reflected? Rate with explanation.
Funnel stage fit: Does the content match its tagged funnel stage? TOFU should educate and build awareness, not assume prior knowledge. MOFU should nurture and compare. BOFU should drive action. Flag misalignment if the content reads like a different stage than it's tagged. Rate with explanation.
Product fit: Does the content clearly connect to the product's value proposition? Is it product-centric or problem-centric? Does it help the reader understand why this product matters for their situation? Rate with explanation.

Source tag: [Source: Content Analysis — from uploaded files, cross-referenced with asset metadata from Internal Data]

Below both tables, provide an AI insight comparing the two: which piece has more relevant topics, which approach (problem-centric vs product-centric) is stronger, and what this means for audience engagement.
